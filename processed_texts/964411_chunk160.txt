Other portfolios may only allow for forward foreign exchange contracts to be used to manage the currency exposure of the portfolio such that the portfolio is fully hedged relative to the benchmark (referred to as a “passive strategy”). In passive strategies, the offset from any unhedged equity positions typically lies in the U.S. dollar currency exposure. Forward foreign exchange contracts are performed against the U.S. dollar and are sometimes entered into in anticipation of equity trades to be executed in the near future as part of the portfolio’s trading program.
