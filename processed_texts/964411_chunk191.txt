An adverse price movement in a derivative position could require cash payments of variation margin by the client portfolio that might in turn require, if there is insufficient cash available in the client portfolio, the sale of the client portfolio’s investments under disadvantageous conditions. Also, there are legal risks involved in using financial derivative instruments which could result in loss due to the unexpected application of a law or regulation or because contracts are not legally enforceable or documented correctly.
