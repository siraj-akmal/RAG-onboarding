Risks Associated with Small Sized Companies. Investments in companies with relatively small market capitalizations generally involve greater risk and price volatility than investments in larger, more established companies because small capitalization companies tend to have younger and more limited product lines, markets and financial resources and could be more dependent on a smaller management group than large capitalization companies. In addition, the equity securities of such companies are typically less liquid
