In a step-out transaction, a block trade is placed with an equity broker with the instruction that the broker execute the entire transaction but “step out” of a portion of the trade in favor of a different broker that has a step-out arrangement with one or more clients of the adviser. The broker with the step-out arrangement receives the commission (or a portion of it) for the stepped-out portion of the trade.
