Trade barriers and other protectionist measures could also have significant adverse effects on the economies of frontier countries as such economies could be largely supported by international trade. Furthermore, frontier market economies may be controlled by a few sectors which could lead to related investments being concentrated into a few sectors. The political climate in such countries can be highly unstable and include the risk of significant government changes, policy changes such as government appropriation or risks of terrorism.
