Directed Brokerage/Commission Recapture Arrangements We define the term “directed brokerage” as an arrangement whereby a broker-dealer agrees to pay client expenses in exchange for commissions. This contrasts with our definition of the term “commission recapture,” which we define as a cash rebate on commissions paid. In both scenarios, the client is receiving benefits from commissions paid on its own trading activity. Under these arrangements, investment advisers do not receive products, cash rebates or services.
