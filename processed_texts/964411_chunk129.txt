Instruments traded in emerging markets can be less liquid than instruments traded in more developed economies and markets and levels of volatility in price movements can also be greater. Moreover, certain emerging markets impose restrictions that specifically impact liquidity, such as restrictions on buying and selling within the same trading day, which could impact our ability to sell securities at a given time and therefore impact the client portfolio.
