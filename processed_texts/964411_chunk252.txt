A short sale is a transaction in which a client portfolio sells a security it does not own in anticipation of a decline in the market price of the security. To affect a short sale, we borrow the security from a prime broker or other financial institution, including custodians that offer “enhanced custody” products or similar products. In borrowing the security to be delivered to the buyer, a client portfolio will become obligated to replace the security
