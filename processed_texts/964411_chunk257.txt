SEC has also adopted a rule that will require public disclosure of securities loans (not including party names), which may be entered into in connection with short sales. If a client portfolio’s short positions or its strategy become generally known as a result of such public filings (or otherwise), it could have a significant effect on our ability to implement its investment strategy. In particular, it would make it more likely that other investors could cause a “short squeeze” in the securities held short by a client portfolio forcing such client portfolio to cover its positions at a loss.
